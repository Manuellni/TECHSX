Frequently Asked Questions

At TECHSX, we understand that navigating the world of cybersecurity can be complex. To help you better understand our services and how we can protect your business, we've compiled a list of frequently asked questions. If you have any additional questions, please don't hesitate to contact us.

What services do you offer?

We offer a wide range of cybersecurity services, including threat detection, incident response, security auditing, penetration testing, network security, endpoint protection, cloud security, and compliance consulting. We also provide tailored solutions to meet the unique needs of your business.

How do I know which plan is right for my business?

Our Basic Plan is ideal for small businesses needing essential protection, the Standard Plan suits medium-sized businesses requiring more advanced services, and the Premium Plan is perfect for larger organizations needing comprehensive protection. If you have specific needs, we also offer custom plans. Contact us to discuss your requirements, and we’ll help you choose the best plan.

What is included in the incident response service?

Our incident response service includes rapid identification and containment of security breaches, investigation of the incident, mitigation of the damage, and support to restore normal operations. We also provide detailed incident reports and recommendations to prevent future incidents.

How often should my business undergo a security audit?

The frequency of security audits depends on your business size, industry, and regulatory requirements. Generally, we recommend annual audits for basic security, bi-annual audits for moderate risk environments, and quarterly audits for high-risk environments. Our team can help you determine the best schedule for your business.

What is penetration testing, and why is it important?

Penetration testing involves simulating cyberattacks on your systems to identify vulnerabilities before malicious actors can exploit them. This proactive approach helps you strengthen your defenses, improve your security posture, and ensure compliance with industry standards.
